---
title: STM32H563
description: Cheapest STM MCU with both Ethernet and CAN-FD; powers the ground-station boards.
---

The H5 is the absolute cheapest microcontroller offered by STM (at the time of writing) that supports Ethernet and CAN-FD. This is the only reason it was picked. That being said, it comes with several other niceties that we take full advantage of:

- 3 PLLs is extraordinarily nice to have and enables a great deal of flexibility when it comes to clock configuration compared to the G4
- The native ethernet MAC allows for futrue use of PTP if needed, such that we can get timestamped measurements without needing an RTC down to sub-millisecond jitter
- Increased compute resources for on-chip testing of drivers, ensuring the logical functionality of our drivers for when it comes time to push them to the G4

The main tradeoff compared to the G4 is I/O complexity, and poor STM32Cube support (which we luckily aren't using now).

## Additional Notes

The getting started AN mentions preferring a 4.7uF cap for both VCAP pins, however, we didn't notice any problems with 2x 2.2uF caps.
